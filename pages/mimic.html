Physical Therapy Training System

My Role: User Experience Designer, Visual Designer
Duration: 3 Months

Current Physical Therapy Guides are hard to follow

The project comes from the study by the PT Department of UofM. There are several problems exsiting in current PT trainings:

  • Materials are static, hard for trainers to follow.
  • No timely feedback, don't know if acting in the right way.
  • Demotivated in the long run.

Inspired by the mataphor of mirror, we tried to use sensors to measure the current gesture of trainers, reflect and compare them with the demo, and provide timely feedbacks to the trainers.

Design of the device

As a start, we created a device to track the basic gestures of the trainer, use Shoulder75 exercise as an example. The device is made up of Lilypad, accelerometers, LED, and Buzzer. The Lilypad can get the 3D location information from the accelerometers, and send signals to the computer to check if the gesture is on the right track compares to our demos. The LED and Buzzer receive the feedback from the computer and make signals to the user. The device is easy to make, without much cost; while provide multi-signals for users to track their gestures, and is in portable size.

The mapping between gesture, device, and interface

The system links the gesture of the trainer to the interface on the screen, the trainer can get multiple feedbacks through the system, they can see the difference between their current gesture and the demo gesture directly through the comparison on the screen. When there is a difference between the demo and current gesture, the LED bulb on that part of the body would flash and the buzzer will vibrate.
When it comes to the training process, I designed a gamification system to help trainer keep motivated. There are 3 highlights of the system:
  • Feedback, instead of amusement, is the most important factor towards the system since trainer can reflect the training persona to themselves.
  • The system combined short-term, e.g. immediate feedback with the long-term feedback, e.g. feedback after a period of time.
  • The training is separated into different stages, focus on different training part, and to avoid simple repeatation.
