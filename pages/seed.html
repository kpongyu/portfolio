SEED—Mobile App to Conquer Culture Shock
  • My Role: User Experience Designer, Visual Designer
  • Duration: 3 Months
  • Client: 2015 CHI Conference
  • Tools & Methods: Sketch 3, Photoshop, InVision

International Students face Culture Shock when studying abroad.

Everyone face some kinds of culture shock when changing the environment, this also applies for international students who left their home country to study aborad, we interviewed 15 international students studying in UofM and 5 American students who has studied abroad and found three major challenges for them to handle the culture shock:

First, People have a variety of topics they want to get better understanding, some interested in professional communications, some interested in daily entertainment topics.
Second, the demands shift from stage to stage, different time interested in different topics.
Third, There is a gap between what they want to know and what they need to know.

The gamification system and storyline

We use "seed" as the metaphor for students' culture knowledge, and developed a gamification system to mimic students development of their cultural knowledge. The growth of students' cultural knowledge is like the growth of a "culture tree", students can develop their knowledge in different aspects and topics, just like different branches of the tree. The input of knowledge is like water to the tree. Students can finially planted their unique culture tree after practice and their personal selections.

Interface Design and implements

The interface present the culture tree as the main screen, and represent the current topics as "bubbles" of the tree. The bubble get filled when user answer the required amount of questions inside the topic. After filling the bubble, the tree "grows" and more topic options are available. We designed four different types of questions for user and created a "garden" to collect and filter new questions.
